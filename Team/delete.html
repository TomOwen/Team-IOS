Deleting Patients, Scans, Lesions, Doctors, Studies

There are instances when it is appropriate to delete one of the TEAM entries such as a Study. Caution should be taken when deleting because there is no undo facility. Your TEAM coordinator may have a TEAM backup of the deleted data but maybe not.

All of the TEAM data is normally displayed in a scrollable table. The following 2 screens show the scrollable tables for Patients and Doctors.



In order to delete a Doctor you would "swipe" with your finger from right to left causing the "Delete" button to appear.



To delete the Doctor you just click on the "Delete" button and the the entry will be removed from both the table and the TEAM data base.

Cautionary Notes

Deleting an entry in TEAM may end up deleting entries associated with that entry.

If you delete an individual lesion in a scan, only that lesion will be deleted.

If you delete a scan date, then the scan date will be deleted AND all the lesions that were entered into that scan date.

If you delete a Patient, all of the patient's scans and all of the entered lesions for that patient will be deleted.

You can delete a study or doctor. Patients assigned to that study and/or doctor will continue to have that study/doctor reported on patient screens and reports.
