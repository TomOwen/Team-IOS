Patient Scan Reports and Study Reports Online

TEAM provides you with the option of storing and displaying patient scan reports and study information.

Just like TEAM allows you the option of storing and displaying images/movies associated with a patient, written reports/information about the scan or study can also be optionally saved and displayed.

If your radiologist has produced a scan report TEAM can store that file and make it available to you via your TEAM server - your TEAM document/image server setup by your TEAM Coordinator.

Also, if you have standardized information or just internal details about a particular study or clinical trial, you can store that document online and have it easily available via TEAM.

Most standard file formats are supported for documents on TEAM (i.e. doc, pdf, txt).

Adding a Patient Scan Report to TEAM



As you are entering scan details (i.e. lesions etc.), TEAM will identify the file name of the scan report that you must use to identify the report. In order to activate the scan reporting capability you need to set the "Online Scan Report Is" switch to "ON". This will then enable the "View Scan Report" button for you to view the scan report after you have uploaded the scan report with the designated file name. The file name consists of the patient ID and scan date(mmddyy) and the document type. (The doc type is set by your TEAM Coordinator via TEAM-ADMIN).

Adding a Study or Clinical Trial Document to TEAM

Study and/or Clinical Trial documents can be very valuable to have available online. In addition to just documents, TEAM allows you to associate an HTML file for the document that gives you the ability to easily point to any internet location. For instance you may want to include a HTML file for a given study that will point you automatically to the NCI Clinical Trial page for that study.


To add a study document to TEAM you can do so on either the ADD Study screen or the Study Detail screen.



The study document file name is up to you to choose. This will give you the flexibility to assign that study document to multiple studies. This would be useful if you had an internal document that applied to more than one study.

The file must be placed on your TEAM document/image server by your TEAM Coordinator.
